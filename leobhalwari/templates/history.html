Leo Clubs (Worldwide)

The first Leo club was founded in 1957 by Jim Graver, the coach of the Abington High School, Pennsylvania baseball team. He was an active member of the Glenside Lions Club. The club was founded with the help of William Ernst, another Lion. It adopted the high school's colors of maroon and gold. The club also created the acronym Leadership, Equality, Opportunity for the word Leo. The word equality was later changed to experience.The club is now run by Mindy Katz. The Leo Club does community work all the time. In 1964 the Leo Club Program became a sponsored program of the Lions Clubs International. It grew beyond Pennsylvania and the United States of America. By 1967 the program had grown to over 200 clubs in 18 countries and become an official youth program of Lions Clubs International. In the following year, the Leo Club Program spread rapidly, more than quadrupling in size, to 918 clubs in 48 countries by the end of 1968.

 Leo Bhalwari Youth

After being involved with an other LEO Club, our charter president LEO LION Arjun Singh Chaudhary had a vision to establish a LEO Club at Bhalwari Area with young blood and energy. He Jointly with MJF LION Archit Shrestha ( Charter President of LIONS Club Of Siddharthanagar Youth ) chartered LEO Club Of Bhalwari Youth (LCOBY) on December 22nd, 2016 with 22 young and energetic personnel from different sectors. We feel very proud to hold the title of 1st club to get established at Bhalwari Region while there were only local clubs. LCOBY has been working on many social sectors from the day of establishment. We have been very successful to encourage people to step forward to give back to society as well, As a result, now we have more than 2 other LEO clubs & 2 new LIONS club in our region, which has eventually provided more opportunities to youths to come forward and overcome their fear and get involved in social activities as well as form their network.

LCOBY has been widely recognized throughout the country because of its capability to work. LCOBY falls under LEO District Council 325 B2 and had been one of the top performing clubs ever since the first year. LCOBY has produced four great leaders, one of which is our honorable Charter President LEO LION Arjun Singh Chaudhary who held the post of president for year 2016-2018 and started the journey of ours. He has worked for LEO District 325 B2 for more than 2 years as a Region 14 Chairperson & as a District Tamer.

Our other admirable leader is LEO Govinda Banjade who contributed his dedication & hard work and followed the path created by our charter president and improvised it further. He held the position of president for year 2018-2019. During his tenure, LCOBY did a record breaking 47 programs and was awarded by the LEO District Council 325 B2 for such commendable record. Presently he is the Area Chief of Area 4 which controls over 40 clubs in total and under his leadership, the face of Area 4 has changed unquestionably for the better.

LEO LION Sushil Gautam is another one of our respectful leaders who worked as a president through year 2019-2020. As we all know, with power comes responsibility and he had an immense pressure to work even better as a successor which was already a great challenge yet he successfully managed to tackle all the tasks and worked furiously for good results. He has worked with LEO District Council 325 B2 as a committee secretary of Suicide Prevention & Drug Awareness Committee.

Our most recent and praiseworthy leader is LEO Bikram Kharel who worked as a president through year 2020-2021. This year was very challenging to him and his team because of the global pandemic of Corona-virus (Covid-19). Even though we couldn’t conduct many of our programs physically, we have still conducted our programs either online or via awareness. However this pandemic came with its own set of problems because of the lockdown and still LCOBY feels very proud to be able to donate and work with people suffering from the lockdown and the virus itself. This year it has been all about safety, awareness, donations and medical checkup.
